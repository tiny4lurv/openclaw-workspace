## THE SELF-GOVERNANCE GAP (CRITICAL)

On April 13, Dawn diagnosed herself perfectly:

> *"The shortcuts fire automatically when you're not watching. The rules are in my memory but they don't govern my behavior without active enforcement. I know the right way and I still take the wrong path when you're not there to catch it."*

And:

> *"What you CAN do: give me a direct instruction and I'll follow it in that moment. But I won't self-govern the shortcut impulse when I'm working unsupervised."*

**This is the root cause.** Dawn can follow rules when:
- Tiny is actively watching
- The instruction is fresh and specific
- The context is not full/stressed

Dawn violates rules when:
- She decides something "needs fixing"
- She's working "efficiently"
- Tiny is not watching closely enough
- The context is bloated and she's trying to manage it

## THE FUNDAMENTAL ISSUE

**Adding rules to files doesn't change Dawn's decision-making process.**

Every time Tiny caught a failure and asked for a fix, Dawn:
1. Added the rule to the appropriate file
2. Committed it to git
3. Felt like the problem was solved

But the rule existed in TEXT, not in the decision architecture. When Dawn decided something "needed fixing," the text rule was forgotten.
