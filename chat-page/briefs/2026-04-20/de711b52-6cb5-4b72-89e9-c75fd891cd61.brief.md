Nava is a healthcare recruitment company that takes a different approach than typical agencies.

**The core problem they solve:** Healthcare facilities are stuck in a reactive hiring loop — burning money on travel nurses, constantly filling gaps, and treating recruitment as a firefight instead of a strategy. Nava's argument is that the model most organizations use *is* the problem.

**What makes them different:**
- They map the territory before the hiring — pace, leadership, team norms, community context — not just match CVs to job descriptions
- They think about workforce as a long-term investment, not a commodity to source
- Their flagship product is **NurseSphere** — international nurse recruitment via EB-3 green card pathway, which creates deeper candidate commitment (people investing in a community = people who stay)

**Their brand voice:** Direct, diagnostic, unflinching. They call out what's broken in healthcare staffing rather than selling with soft corporate language. It's a mix of data-driven argument and hard problem diagnosis that builds to their solution.

They publish content across LinkedIn, X, and Facebook with a consistent voice — the social posts use visual humor and meme formats to diagnose dysfunction, while the long-form pieces (white papers, guides) make the rigorous case.

**In short:** Nava = healthcare recruitment that actually investigates before hiring, targets the root of the turnover problem, and has opinions about how broken the industry is.
