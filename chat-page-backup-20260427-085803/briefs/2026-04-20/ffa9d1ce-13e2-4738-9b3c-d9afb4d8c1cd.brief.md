Based on Nava's content, the key differences:

**The Speed-vs-Quality Trap**
Typical agencies optimize for *time-to-fill* — a metric Nava calls out as structurally incentivizing bad fits. Push candidates fast, collect the placement fee, move on. Nava explicitly rejects this model.

**Vetting Approach**
- **Typical agencies**: Match resume keywords to job descriptions. Transactional. Blind to soft skills and cultural fit.
- **Nava**: "Relational framework" — assesses EQ, resilience, communication style, motivations through rapport-building. The 3-vs-500 ratio: a real agency screens hundreds down to 3 worth interviewing. A job board dumps 500 resumes and makes *you* do the screening.

**Business Model**
- Typical agencies: Transactional placements, upfront costs, success tied to *filling the role* not keeping the hire
- Nava: Co-created Role Briefs, no upfront costs, performance-based agreements where Nava's success is tied to **long-term retention**

**Proactive vs. Reactive**
Most agencies wait for you to have an urgent gap. Nava frames itself around building talent pipelines *before* crises hit — which is the only way to avoid the revolving door

**The Real Issue Nava Names**
> "It is a system built to manage a revolving door, not to build a stable team" — describing the typical agency model

Typical agencies profit from turnover. Nava's pitch is that their model only wins if the hire *stays*.
