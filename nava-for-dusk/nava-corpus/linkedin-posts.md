## Post 3 — Apr 9, 2026 — Continuity / Merry-Go-Rounds

A travelling nurse reaches week seven of their contract and finally understands the culture and where the best karaoke in town is. Week nine, they've started anticipating which physicians prefer which workflows.

Then week thirteen arrives, and they leave.

𝗧𝗵𝗶𝘀 𝗵𝗮𝗽𝗽𝗲𝗻𝘀 𝗹𝗶𝗸𝗲 𝗰𝗹𝗼𝗰𝗸𝘄𝗼𝗿𝗸, but not because nurses want to leave. Some negotiate to come back. Some choose their next contract carefully. But many leave because this is how to make a living in this industry.

The 13-week reset is a structural feature of the travel staffing model. Agencies profit on volume: new placements, new contracts, new orientation gaps. A nurse who knows your unit intimately is a nurse who's harder to place. Familiarity breeds comfort, and places unknown come with uncertainty and social anxiety. So the system moves them before they feel at home.

𝗬𝗼𝘂𝗿 𝘂𝗻𝗶𝘁 𝗽𝗮𝘆𝘀 𝘁𝗵𝗲 𝗽𝗿𝗶𝗰𝗲: hand-off errors, orientation costs, the institutional knowledge that never accumulates because it keeps walking out the door.

We built Nava differently. Continuity requires someone who stays, so we structure around that from day one:
 • Truth at placement,
 • Follow-up after arrival,
 • Relationships that outlast the contract cycle.
