# Writing Style Analysis - Executive Summary

## Overview
This analysis examines the writing styles of 6 authors across 77 articles in the AbroadWorks corpus using established stylometric features from linguistic science.

## Key Findings

### Author Contributions
- **Sherry T.**: 25 articles (32.5%) - Most frequent contributor
- **Tiny Manyonga**: 23 articles (29.9%) - Second most frequent
- **David Landau**: 17 articles (22.1%) - Technical specialist
- **Yhen Villas**: 7 articles (9.1%) - Engagement specialist
- **Paul Sial**: 4 articles (5.2%) - Vocabulary expert
- **Mike Roston**: 1 article (1.3%) - Specialist in remote work topics

### Style Champions
- **Highest Engagement**: Yhen Villas (uses personal pronouns and interactive elements most effectively)
- **Richest Vocabulary**: Mike Roston (single article shows exceptional lexical diversity)
- **Most Accessible**: Mike Roston (highest readability score - easiest to understand)
- **Most Consistent Style**: Mike Roston (lowest sentence length variance - most balanced)
- **Most Conversational**: Sherry T. (lowest function word ratio - least formal/style)

### Content Strategy Insights
1. **For Engagement**: Use Yhen Villas' style - personal connection markers, questions, exclamations
2. **For Clarity**: Use Mike Roston's approach - simple sentences, common words, clear structure
3. **For Technical Topics**: David Landau's style excels at making complex topics accessible
4. **For Volume & Versatility**: Tiny Manyonga demonstrates sustainable, varied output
5. **For Vocabulary Depth**: Paul Sial shows careful, varied word choice

### Recommendations for Writing Team
1. **Match style to content type**: Use accessibility metrics for technical topics, engagement for announcements
2. **Develop style guides**: Create templates based on proven patterns from top performers
3. **Cross-train writers**: Share techniques across authors (e.g., Sherry's engagement techniques with David's clarity)
4. **Monitor evolution**: Track how individual styles change over time for development planning
