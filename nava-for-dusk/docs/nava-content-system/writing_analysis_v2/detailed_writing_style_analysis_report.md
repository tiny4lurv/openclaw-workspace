## Methodology Overview

This analysis uses established **stylometric features** from linguistic research and authorship attribution studies. The metrics selected are those proven to be effective in distinguishing authorial writing styles.

### Key Metric Categories:

1. **Lexical Features**: Word choice, vocabulary richness, formality indicators
2. **Syntactic Features**: Sentence structure, complexity, length patterns
3. **Character-Level Features**: Punctuation usage, character frequencies
4. **Function Words**: Grammatical words (the, of, and, etc.) - highly indicative of author style

## Why These Metrics Work

Research in stylometry shows that authors have unconscious, consistent patterns in:
- **Function word usage** (articles, prepositions, pronouns) - these are stylistic fingerprints
- **Preferred sentence lengths and structures**
- **Vocabulary choices and richness**
- **Punctuation habits**
- **Readability and formality preferences**

## Metrics Explained: What Each Tells Us About Style

### 📏 **Length & Structure Metrics**
**Average Sentence Length**: Longer sentences often indicate formal, complex thinking; shorter sentences suggest direct, conversational style.
**Sentence Length Variability**: High variance indicates flexible, adaptive style; low variance suggests consistent, rhythmic patterns.
**Average Paragraph Length**: Reflects idea development - longer paragraphs = more developed arguments.

### 📚 **Lexical & Vocabulary Metrics**
**Type-Token Ratio**: Measures vocabulary diversity (unique words/total words). Higher = richer vocabulary.
**Hapax Legomena Ratio**: Percentage of words used only once. Higher = more experimental/varied vocabulary.
**Function Word Rate**: Usage of 'the', 'of', 'and', etc. - surprisingly consistent per author and hard to fake.
**Personal Pronouns**: 'I', 'we', 'my' - high usage indicates personal, engaging style; low = objective, formal.

### 🔤 **Readability & Sophistication**
**Flesch Reading Ease**: 0-100 scale (higher = easier to read). Based on sentence length and syllable count.
**Vocabulary Sophistication**: Latinate endings (-tion, -ment) vs Anglo-Saxon roots indicates formality level.

### 📝 **Punctuation & Style**
**Commas**: Indicate complex, clausal sentences and careful thought.
**Questions**: Suggest interactive, engaging, exploratory style.
**Exclamations**: Indicate enthusiasm, emphasis, or informal tone.
**Colons/Semicolons**: Show complex, structured, formal writing.

### ⚖️ **Why Engagement Seemed Low Before**
The previous 'engagement score' was simplistic. Proper engagement analysis looks at:
- **Personal pronouns** (I, we, my) - indicates direct reader connection
- **Questions** - invites reader participation and reflection
- **Exclamations** - shows enthusiasm and emotional connection
- **Conversational markers** - contractions, informal language
