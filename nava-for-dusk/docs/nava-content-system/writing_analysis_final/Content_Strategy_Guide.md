### When You Need Controversy/Timely Impact → Yhen Villas
- His question rate (0.49 per 100w) and personal pronouns (0.034) create engaging, conversational controversy
- Example topics: Political/policy takes, industry shake-ups, trending debates

### When You Need Technical Depth → Paul Sial
- Highest vocabulary richness (TTR: 0.472) and complexity (Flesch: 33.7)
- Example topics: Deep analysis, technical comparisons, comprehensive guides

### When You Need Accessibility → David Landau
- Best readability (Flesch: 43.16) and natural patterns
- Example topics: Explainer content, beginner guides, industry trends

### When You Need Volume/Reliability → Tiny Manyonga or Sherry T.
- Combined: 48 articles, 96,469 words (67% of corpus)

### When You Need Structured How-To → Sherry T.

## Content Calendar Recommendations

### High-Volume Writers (Tiny, Sherry)
- Assign 60% of regular blog content
- Focus on SEO-friendly, evergreen topics
- Leverage high output for content frequency

### Specialist Writers (Paul, David, Yhen)
- Assign 40% of content, focusing on their strengths
- Paul: Technical deep-dives
- David: Accessibility-first explainers
- Yhen: Timely controversy, trending topics

### Seasonal/Timely Content
- Yhen for rapid-response content (controversy, policy, trends)
- Sherry for comprehensive guides (evergreen)
- David for trend analysis (industry shifts)

## Key Metrics to Track

### Engagement Indicators
- Question usage (target: 0.25-0.40 per 100 words for engagement balance)
- Personal pronouns (target: 0.025-0.035 for personal connection)
- Exclamation marks (target: 0.15-0.25 for emphasis without excess)

### Quality Indicators
- Readability (target: 38-45 Flesch for business content)
- Sentence variety (variance: 85-115 for rhythm)
- Lexical diversity (TTR: 0.40-0.47 for sophistication without obscurity)

### Output Indicators
- Weekly word count by author
- Topic diversity per writer
- Publishing consistency
