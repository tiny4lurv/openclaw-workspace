## Strategic Summary

**What this article is really about:** Job boards are not strategy — they generate volume, not fit. Healthcare hiring requires precision that job boards are structurally incapable of delivering. The real failure isn't technical; it's conceptual. Job boards externalize costs that healthcare leaders still pay, and the hidden time tax drains recruiter bandwidth while producing revolving-door turnover.

**Core argument arc:**

1. **The Polycrisis Context:** 100K+ nurses left 2019–2022; 600K more expected by 2027. Nearly half of healthcare executives considering stepping down within a year (AMN Healthcare). Urgency is undeniable — but ramping up job board ads is insufficient.

2. **The Misalignment Problem:**
   - Job boards built for high-turnover, low-specialization roles — not clinical care
   - Healthcare requires: acuity alignment, shift expectations, team culture, behavioral fit
   - These variables are not what job boards are designed to capture
   - "Reliance on job boards as the primary engine of healthcare hiring is like using a temporary staffing agency to build a trauma team"

3. **Three Problems with Job Boards:**
   - **Résumé Overload:** 100 blind submissions ≠ 100 strong candidates. Most unvetted for licenses, preferences, geographic accuracy
   - **Filter Failures:** Shift preference, behavioral alignment, travel flexibility get buried or not tracked. Recruiters waste hours chasing shadows
   - **Ghosting Cycle:** Click-to-apply reduces friction but also commitment. Nearly half of applicants drop out after applying due to lack of engagement (Crosschq)

4. **The Tool-Migration Trap:** Moving to adjacent tactics (referral bonuses, social ads, recruitment marketing) often just replicates transactional logic:
   - Social ads boost visibility but rarely vet for clinical nuance
   - Referral bonuses drive volume with confirmation bias
   - "Dashboards light up, pipelines look full, but placements still fall apart after two months"

5. **The Hidden Time Tax:** Every resume submitted through a job board takes time to open, scan, cross-check. Hundreds of submissions = days sifting through clutter. Burnout happens in the inbox first.

6. **Why Compatibility Cannot Be Automated:**
   - Real compatibility requires: understanding work environments, behavioral alignment, long-term retention signals
   - None visible on a resume
   - "No job board is asking the questions that reveal them"
   - Shift Shock cited: mismatched acuity, unwanted float assignments, incompatible shift structures are top reasons clinicians walk away

7. **The Coordination Gap:** Roles posted without clarity on acuity levels, shift intensity, unit culture. Poor communication between recruiters and clinical teams stalls process.

8. **Nava's Position:** "We use job boards too. But not like most do."
   - Recruiter-level access across major platforms for targeted search + personalized outreach at scale
   - Messaging dozens/hundreds of candidates per role
   - Combined with clinical insight and relationship-building
   - Job boards find a name; conversation confirms a match
   - "It is not magic. It is just ownership."
