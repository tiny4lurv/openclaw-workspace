## Post-by-Post Breakdown

### X01-X06 — "Crisis by Design" Series (Sep 8, 2025)
Six posts using meme format to diagnose systemic healthcare staffing failure:
- **X01:** "This Is Fine" dog in burning room — US ~200K nurse shortage, boards approve 2-3% raises, "hands are tied." Fire keeps growing.
- **X02:** 3% raise vs 6% inflation = 3% effective pay cut every year. After a decade, experienced nurses leave. Now vacancy can't be filled at any price.
- **X03:** Grim Reaper holding help-wanted sign — "Hospitals don't struggle to hire because there aren't enough nurses. They struggle because they hire the same way they've always hired." The Grim Reaper is already inside the boardroom.
- **X04:** Woman Yelling at Cat — "CFOs to agencies: we need to cut costs. Also CFOs: approves $50M in new administrative overhead." The people yelling loudest about recruitment costs often approved the model creating the problem.
- **X05:** Empty boardroom — same generic job ad on same three job boards. Six months later: surprise, nothing changed.
- **X06:** The irony — "Crisis by Design" most-viewed content this quarter. "Everyone recognizes the problem but no one believes it can be solved differently. It can. But it requires doing something other than what got you here."

### X07 — "Crisis by Design Part 5: The Door" (From x-linkedin-paired-corpus)
**Premise:** So what actually works?
**Argument:** Not more money (travel nurses get 2-4x staff rates and it hasn't solved anything). Not better job ads. Not "culture initiatives" while running skeleton crews. What works: change the question entirely. Instead of "How do we fill this shift?" → "How do we build a workforce that doesn't need filling every 13 weeks?" "That's the door most organizations won't walk through. Because it requires admitting that the model you're using is the problem."

### X08 — "Numbers Game: UNO Draw 25" (Oct 14, 2025)
**Premise:** Most processes: 1. Post job ad → 2. Screen 200 CVs → 3. Conduct 15 interviews → 4. Make 1 offer → 5. Candidate declines. Nava's process: 1. Identify candidates who fit → 2. Pre-vet → 3. You meet 3-5 max → 4. Offer accepted.
**Argument:** "We skip the game. You skip the 200 CVs."

### X09 — "Perfect on Paper: Class Types" (Dec 11, 2025)
**Premise:** Resume: License active, 10 years experience, Med-surg certified. What resume doesn't show: refused last three orientation requests, collected four sign-on bonuses in two years, lasted 11 months at three consecutive facilities.
**Argument:** "Class Types is about learning to read between the lines."

### X10 — "Clinical DNA" (Dec 18, 2025)
**Premise:** What matters (not on resume): how nurse communicates at 3am when attending unavailable; how they carry themselves during rapid response; whether their defaults lean toward caution or confidence; how they talk about patients when no one's listening.
**Argument:** "You can't screen for this on a piece of paper."

### X11 — "Staff vs Agency: Mother Ignoring Kid Meme" (Dec 23, 2025)
**Premise:** "$100/hr travelling nurse vs $60/hr staff nurse" — sentiment shared by permanent nurses feeling betrayed. Management is trapped: matching rate triggers Step Scale reset, $2M agency bill vs $15M fixed cost explosion.
**Argument:** "They aren't choosing strangers over staff; they are choosing survival over bankruptcy. But we found a way out."
**Connects to:** Reclaimix product.

### X12 — "Sign-On Bonus: Distracted Boyfriend" (Mar 3, 2026)
**Premise:** For 90% of candidates, $5K sign-on bonus + standard waiting period = fair trade. For veteran nurse with 10+ years, $5K is insult — waiting period = 3 months before they can afford rent.
**Argument:** "When this policy drives an elite clinician away, you aren't saving $5K. You're triggering $72,900-$5,900 in lost revenue every day + 50% labor inflation + $64,000 replacement bill." (Note: numbers differ in different posts — likely A/B testing or different scenarios)
**Follow-up:** "Don't let 3-star rules outdo your 5-star talent."

### X13 — "False Finish Line" (Mar 3 / Mar 22, 2026)
**Premise:** Most teams think race is over when offer accepted. Reality: counteroffers, credentialing delays, second thoughts, onboarding friction. Between "yes" and Day 60 is where many placements quietly fail.
**Argument:** "If your agent disappears once they get paid and the solution is a 'free replacement' within 90 days, Nava will feel very different."

### X14 — "Merry-Go-Rounds" (Apr 9, 2026)
**Premise:** Nurse reaches week 7, understands culture and where best karaoke is. Week 9, anticipating physician preferences. Week 13, they leave. "This happens like clockwork, but not because nurses want to leave."
**Argument:** "The 13-week reset is a structural feature of the travel staffing model. Agencies profit on volume: new placements, new contracts, new orientation gaps. A nurse who knows your unit intimately is a nurse who's harder to place." "Your unit pays the price: hand-off errors, orientation costs, institutional knowledge that never accumulates because it keeps walking out the door."
**Nava differentiator:** "We structure around continuity from day one: Truth at placement, Follow-up after arrival, Relationships that outlast the contract cycle."

### X15 — "Stop calling them soft skills" (March 2026)
**Thread (5 tweets):**
"We need to stop calling them 'soft skills.' What they actually are: the skills that determine whether a nurse thrives or implodes in your specific unit."
- How they handle being asked to do something they weren't trained for
- How they communicate under pressure at 3am
- Whether they escalate or white-knuckle through a crisis
- How they talk about patients when no one is listening
"These aren't soft. They're load-bearing."

### X16 — "Does your recruitment process feel like a transaction, not a relationship?" (March 2026)
**Premise:** Post → apply → screen → offer → accept. No trust, no follow-up, no investment in whether they thrive.
**Argument:** "That's mail-sorting, not recruitment."

### X17 — "The Renting Problem" (DRAFT / Mar 25, 2026)
**Premise:** "You've been renting your workforce. Every 13 weeks, the agency sends someone new. Same facility. Same patients. New person figuring out where the supplies are. That's not staffing. That's a subscription service for instability."
**Math:** Agency nurse $102/hr, 100% turnover every 13 weeks vs. Permanent nurse $41/hr, 16% turnover annually. Plus 32-65% agency markup.
**Solution:** "Reclaimix identifies the strong agency nurses already in your building and converts them — no interviews, no onboarding, no disruption. Same person. About $100k in savings per nurse, per year. Stop renting strangers."

### X18 — "Oncology CNA" (Apr 6, 2026)
**Author:** Nava Corporate
**Premise:** "Eight patients. One CNA. In a rehab unit, typical Tuesday. In an oncology unit, that's a crisis." Story starts in Wichita but $39 billion industry built to make sure acuity gaps keep happening.
**Argument:** Travel nursing is now a permanent feature, not crisis response. Market hit $39 billion, projected $72 billion by 2033. "That growth isn't a sign the system is solving its staffing problem, but a symptom the system is more dependent on the thing causing the problem."
**Cycle:** Hospitals pay 32-65% agency markups → permanent nurses leave because travelers earn 25% more → hospitals can't afford raises to bring them back → they travel more → cycle feeds itself.
**Conclusion:** "We're spending our way into a hole we're still digging. This can end with structural change. Which is why we built NurseSphere."

### X19 — "Green Card, Not Visa" (DRAFT / Mar 25, 2026)
**Premise:** Most international recruitment = visa. NurseSphere = green card. Visa expires, renewable, temporary. Green card = permanent resident, cannot be outbid. 6,000-hour commitment, 3 years.
**Argument:** "The only staffing model built for stability in an industry designed around churn."
