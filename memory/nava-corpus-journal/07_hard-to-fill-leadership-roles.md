## Strategic Summary

**What this article is really about:** Hard-to-fill leadership roles in healthcare require discipline, precision, and active sourcing — not job board postings. The article makes the case for strategic prioritization, role precision, market-aware outreach, and balanced internal/external pipeline development.

**Core argument arc:**

1. **The Scarcity Is Structural** — HBS research: organizations relying on last-minute/informal hiring face prolonged vacancies, elevated turnover, costly mis-hires. Supply of leaders with operational depth + transformation capability lags demand across industries.

2. **Healthcare Adds Complexity** — Roles are shifting in scope (consolidating responsibilities, creating new positions tied to analytics/AI/value-based care). "Buy not build" mindset leaves orgs vulnerable — everyone chases the same scarce digital/transformation skills.

3. **Prioritization and Succession Need Discipline** — Apply Sullivan's framework: classify roles by strategic impact (clinical operations, quality, compliance, revenue cycle, nursing leadership, multi-site admin). Succession planning is not accidental — it's the result of years of deliberate or informal development.

4. **Define the Role With Precision** — Senior leaders ask: what problem am I being asked to solve? Need: strategic objectives for 12-24 months, decisions owned, constraints inherited, support expected. Context matters — healthcare roles sit at intersection of clinical ops, finance, workforce, community.

5. **Market-Aware Sourcing Strategy** — Strongest candidates are usually not in active search mode. Effective outreach requires: structured market map, targeted direct outreach, professional association networks, selective visibility platforms. Personalization is essential — most senior leaders receive generic outreach.

6. **Mission and Leadership Culture** — Mission alignment carries more weight in healthcare than in other sectors. Compensation matters but doesn't determine outcome when the role affects patient care. Structured onboarding, coaching, upskilling needed — especially digital/AI readiness.

7. **Balance External Hiring With Internal Capability Building** — Over-indexing on external hires struggles to build cohesive leadership culture. Reserve external searches for roles requiring specialized experience or external credibility. Cross-functional rotations, stretch assignments, structured development for internal leaders. Same competency model for internal and external candidates.

8. **Strengthen Assessment, Reduce Risk** — Behavioral evidence, scenario discussions, psychometric evaluation, multi-stakeholder reference checks. Structured onboarding plan. Healthcare executives step into complex environments — clear alignment in first months is critical.
