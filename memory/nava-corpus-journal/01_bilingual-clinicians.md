## Strategic Summary

**What this article is really about:** The interpreter compliance model is a workforce strategy failure. It checks the legal box while actively destroying clinical quality, operational efficiency, and staff retention.

**Core argument arc:**

1. **The Clinical Blindspot** — 3 in 4 diagnoses depend on dialogue. Without shared language, even brilliant clinicians operate with incomplete data. The "excellent mind" fallacy: medical brilliance can't transfer through an interpreter.

2. **The Black Box** — Triadic (three-person) communication filters hesitation, cultural idioms, and "shadow data" (patients won't disclose barriers to an interpreter). False agreement. No therapeutic partnership possible.

3. **The Technician Trap** — Clinicians become data-entry technicians. Joy of practice is stripped. Emotional feedback loop severed. This isn't burnout from workload — it's moral injury from systematic ineffectiveness.

4. **The Translation Tax** — 56% efficiency loss. Variable interpreter costs vs. fixed bilingual salary. The more patients you see, the more you pay. In a PPS environment, this suppresses revenue directly.

5. **Moral Injury as Retention Killer** — Chronic background anxiety (Did they understand the insulin dose?) erodes mission-driven clinicians until they leave. The effort-reward imbalance is the primary predictor of burnout in safety-net settings.

6. **The Concordance Strategy** — Bilingualism as core clinical competency, not bonus skill. Cultural concordance builds mission-fit (providers from the community), creates sticky retention, and generates fixed-cost efficiency that compounds over time.
