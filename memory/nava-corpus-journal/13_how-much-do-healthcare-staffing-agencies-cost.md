## Strategic Summary

**What this article is really about:** "How much do agencies charge?" is the wrong question. The right question is what model you're buying. Contingency and retained search models are structurally misaligned — they incentivize speed over quality, transfer all financial risk to the client, and the 90-day guarantee is a sales feature, not real protection. The value-aligned model (zero upfront, retention-based billing) is the only structure that creates true partnership.

**Core argument arc:**

1. **The Wrong Question:** "How much do agencies charge?" ignores the real cost. Single RN turnover = $61,100+. Unfilled physician role = ~$8,000/day lost revenue. Fee structure = agency's business philosophy in financial form.

2. **Traditional Fee Models:**

   **Contingency (15-30% of first-year salary):**
   - "No cure, no pay" — seems attractive
   - Multiple agencies competing for same search
   - FLaw: incentivizes speed over quality — race to present first available candidate
   - Deep vetting (team dynamics, behavioral fit, long-term goals) sacrificed to close before competitor
   - Result: mis-hires, trust deterioration

   **Retained Search (25-35%, thirds):**
   - For executive/critical roles (CNO, DON)
   - Paid upfront + at candidate slate + at start
   - Exclusive, comprehensive service
   - Better quality but high upfront commitment

3. **Hidden Costs and Misaligned Incentives:**
   - Contingency: agency earns fee at START DATE, not after integration
   - Client pays before performance proven
   - If candidate quits at month 2, facility paid twice (failed hire + replacement)
   - RN vacancy costs $22,000–$62,000/month in lost revenue + overtime
   - 1% change in RN turnover = ~$289,000 annual impact for hospital system
   - Travel/contract clinicians cost up to 160% of average rate

4. **The 90-Day Guarantee Fallacy:**
   - Sounds like protection but is a sales feature
   - Doesn't cover sunk costs: salary paid, onboarding resources, manager time
   - Ignores "replacement fatigue" — instability, morale damage, second disruptive hiring cycle
   - True cost of bad hire is organizational instability, not the replacement credit

5. **Value-Aligned Partnership Model:**
   - Core principle: agency's financial success tied to candidate's long-term success
   - KPI shifts from "Did we fill the role?" to "Did we place a long-term asset?"
   - **Zero upfront fees:** Agency demonstrates confidence, shoulders initial risk
   - **Retention-based fee:** Full fee earned only after successful integration
     - Nava: 30 days for floor staff, 60 days for management roles
   - Agency wins only when client truly wins
