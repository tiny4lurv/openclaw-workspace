## Strategic Summary

**What this corpus is really about:** Nava's Facebook posts use visual humor and meme formats to diagnose healthcare staffing dysfunction. The tone is more irreverent than LinkedIn or X — plays to Facebook's more casual scroll behavior. Targets healthcare administrators and managers who recognize the absurdity in their own systems.

## Post-by-Post Breakdown

### FB01 — "WHAT RECRUITING AGENCIES SEE vs. WHAT NAVA SEES" (Mar 22, 2026)
**Format:** Two-panel visual comparison.
**"What recruiting agencies see":** Open Order: 1 Registered Nurse → RNs, LPNs, CNAs (chaotic hospital scene).
**"What Nava sees":** HIGH-TECH ER / CARE HOME LOUNGE / DIAGNOSTIC LENS / PEDIATRIC WARD → "Context is clinical DNA."
**Argument:** "If your recruitment partner receives your job specs and responds with a bunch of CVs, fire them." They decided your unit's clinical DNA is irrelevant. "Universal license fallacy" — belief that license = universal key that fits every clinical lock. "This is the primary cause of clinical friction and the 90 Day cliff."
**Key line:** "Do not hire the CV. Hire the people behind the professional qualifications."

### FB02 — "The $15,600 Entry Fee" (Mar 3, 2026)
**Format:** Text post with scenario.
**Scenario:** "Star" clinical leader, $5K sign-on bonus, turns down for competitor not offering bonus. What are they missing?
**Argument:** 90-day waiting period + 60-day notice period = $15,600 "entry fee" to work for you. "A massive personal risk they aren't being distracted by the competitor they are choosing the facility that respects their reality as a human."
**Nava differentiator:** "We provide the deep vetting that internal HR can't have bandwidth for. When we ask you to make a strategic exception for the clinical leader, we're accountable for the decision." "Every single one of them made it clear: your facility isn't just a job. It's a place that respects their worth."

### FB03 — "Agency Nurse $100/hr vs. Staff Nurse $40/hr" (Dec 23, 2023)
**Format:** Visual comparison (hospital admin / agency nurse $100/hr with pizza party budget / staff nurse $40/hr).
**Argument:** Staff nurse feels ignored, but admin is trapped. Matching agency rate triggers Step Scale reset → $100K+ fixed cost explosion vs. $20 variable recurring cost. "They're not ignoring you because they want to — they're using the agency as a life raft to keep the building open."
**Note:** "It's unsustainable, the system is broken but not unfixed. That's why we'll health Reclaimik. Watch this space." (Early Reclaimix mention, before product was named correctly)

### FB04 — "POV: You hired the 'Perfect' on Paper candidate" (Dec 11, 2023)
**Format:** Four-panel meme using character cards (Dr. Derek Shepherd, Sherlock Holmes, [unnamed third character described as "Stabilizes with duct tape"]).
**Scenario:** "Code Red" shortage at safety net urban hospital. Four candidates:
- **The Specialist:** Ivy League, 5-Star Experience. Reality: Asks about sparkling water, quits Week 2 when Philips machine jams. "Too stressful."
- **The Consultant:** Top Efficiency, Zero Errors. Reality: Canceled waiting room in 2hrs. Insulted family members. "Technically correct, legally a disaster."
- **The Maverick:** Genius IQ, Diagnostic Wizard. Reality: Refuses to start because "paperwork is for mortals." Scares new grads. Currently explaining to CNO why he's smarter than Radiology tech.
- **The Veteran:** Decades of experience, classic image of dedicated professional. Reality: Asked for USB-A to HDMI cable because "hospital AV system is ancient." Compatibility: 98%.
**Argument:** "Recruitment feels like a strategy game where the odds are usually rigged. You see the Resume. You miss the Class Type (Reality)."

## Platform Notes

**Facebook voice:** Most irreverent of all platforms. Uses humor, memes, and visual satire more aggressively. Healthcare managers/administrators scroll Facebook differently than they scroll LinkedIn — more personal time, less professional scrutiny.

**Engagement pattern:** Links back to blog articles (Crisis by Design series). Visual posts drive traffic to long-form content.

**Commenting behavior:** Multiple posts show "Comment as Tiny Dawn [Gummers / Summers / Gunner / Dummier]" — tiny variations suggesting testing or different page roles.

## What's Distinctive

**FB04 (Class Types / "Perfect on Paper")** is the most developed visual post — uses character card format that plays across platforms (LinkedIn/X had the same concept in different formats). The Specialist/Consultant/Maverick/Veteran taxonomy is the same as LI06/X06 but with different character framing.

**FB07 ("HIRE SOMEONE WHO LEAVES IN 3 MONTHS")** is the most clever flowchart — makes the entire reactive hiring model visible in three steps.

**FB03 ($100/hr agency vs. $40/hr staff)** is the earliest Reclaimix mention — shows product was being developed even in 2023. "Watch this space" for Reclaimix.

**FB05 (New Grad RN)** is the most human story — six weeks of failure because of onboarding mismatch is a scenario every nurse manager recognizes.

**FB13 (None of them are a good fit)** is the most quoted short post — 74 characters that capture the job board paradox perfectly.
