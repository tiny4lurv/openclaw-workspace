## Strategic Summary

**What this article is really about:** Healthcare leaders use the wrong scorecard to evaluate agencies — time-to-fill and cost-per-hire are transactional metrics that create perverse incentives. The right framework is Quality of Hire (QoH): Role Brief adherence, 90-day and 1-year retention, and Day Zero candidate experience. True accountability requires retention-based fee structures.

**Core argument arc:**

1. **The "Speed-Over-Quality" Trap:**
   - 43% of clients cite time-to-fill as most important metric
   - This optimizes for transaction velocity, not durable placements
   - Recruiters push "good enough" candidates, glossing over culture/acuity misalignment
   - PubMed research links churn-and-burn cycle to higher patient falls and medication errors
   - Root cause of early turnover: "disappointment about nursing reality" (mismatched expectations) + "unsatisfactory psychological climate" (poor unit culture)

2. **The Cost-Per-Hire Fallacy:**
   - Agency RN fee: $14,000–$23,400
   - True cost of RN turnover: $61,000+
   - Average hospital: $3.9M–$5.7M annually in turnover costs
   - penny wise, pound foolish at enterprise level

3. **The 90-Day Accountability Gap:**
   - 57% of all healthcare turnover occurs WITHIN first 90 days
   - 90-day guarantee is a transactional loophole — agency collects fee, facility absorbs failure
   - High proportion of failures happen AFTER guarantee lapses
   - "Free replacement" doesn't cover: disrupted patient care, lost team morale, wasted training resources

4. **The Strategic Scorecard — Quality of Hire (QoH):**

   **Foundational: Role Brief Adherence**
   - Co-created document aligning HR, operations, and agency
   - Measure: "fit-to-brief" ratio of submitted candidates
   - High ratio = true consultant; low ratio = resume pusher

   **Core: 90-Day and 1-Year Retention**
   - 31.9% of all new RNs leave within first year
   - 1-year retention = true test of quality hire
   - Without this metric, agency can place 20 clinicians, collect 20 fees, even if 15 quit in 6 months

   **Leading: Day Zero Candidate Experience**
   - 50% of job seekers have declined an offer due to poor experience
   - Measure: candidate feedback scores, time-to-credentialing, Day One readiness
   - Early warning system for agency professionalism

5. **Activating the Scorecard:**
   - Quarterly Business Review (QBR) — forum for strategic dialogue
   - Agenda: 90-day retention rate + plan to improve, root cause analysis, co-developed solutions
   - Cannot hold agency accountable for retention if financial incentives remain transactional
   - Retention-based fee: Nava's model = pay at 30 days (floor staff) / 60 days (management)

## What's Distinctive

The three-layer scorecard (foundational, core, leading) is a useful organizational structure — it gives leaders a sequence for evaluation, starting with the intake process before moving to retention outcomes.

The 57% within-90-days statistic is devastating in context — it means the industry standard 90-day guarantee covers the exact period when most failures happen, which means the guarantee is designed to be used frequently while appearing protective.
