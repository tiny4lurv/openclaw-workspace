## Strategic Summary

**What this article is really about:** Ambiguity is the root cause of preventable costs and poor outcomes in healthcare hiring. The fix is operational discipline: define the role precisely (Role Brief), control Day Zero, and track three visible metrics. This is Nava's operational framework for hiring without guesswork.

**Core argument arc:**

1. **Ambiguity Is the Root Cause** — Chronic healthcare hiring problems (prolonged vacancies, misaligned recruiter incentives, poor practices) all trace back to ambiguity. Lack of clarity enables sustained inefficiencies.

2. **Orientation Promises vs. Staffing Realities** — Story: new grad RN expected structured orientation, instead was thrown onto floor alone due to understaffing → crisis in confidence, potential burnout/early exit. Better-informed candidates would prepare differently.

3. **The Coordination Problem** — Recruiters sell one version of the job, managers assume another, support teams join too late. Candidates experience "sequence of avoidable starts and stops." This is not a pipeline problem — it's a coordination problem.

4. **The Case for Clarity:**
   - Role defined as actually performed on the unit
   - First week planned, not improvised
   - Internal teams and candidate both understand what success looks like at 30 and 90 days

5. **Two Key Tools:**
   - **Role Brief:** Single source of truth for the job — why the role exists, handful of behaviors that drive outcomes, context clinician will face, systems used, non-negotiables, what progress looks like at 30/90 days. Written by unit lead + preceptor + recruiter.
   - **Day Zero:** From verbal acceptance to first safe shift. HR verifies credentials before arrival, IT confirms access/equipment, unit lead confirms schedule/assigns preceptor, 3-shift progression (read-only → shadow → partial assignment).

6. **Pre-boarding:** Engagement period during Day Zero to ready the hire. Documents, logistics email, early mentor/preceptor introductions. 11% better retention than no pre-boarding.

7. **Three Metrics That Matter:**
   - **Offer to Start Days:** Calendar days from verbal acceptance to first shift. Goal: shorter, predictable window.
   - **Start Adherence:** % of accepted offers that convert to Day One arrival. Goal: steady rise.
   - **30- and 90-day Retention:** % still on staff at day 30 and 90. Goal: stability through first quarter.

8. **Nava's Practices (not promises):**
   - Outcome-tied billing: 30-60 day success before payment
   - Proactive sourcing guided by Role Brief consultation
   - Scheduling + candidate contact that keeps momentum
   - Compliance handled up front
   - Reclaimix: converts agency nurses already in building to permanent staff (~$100K savings per nurse per year)
