### $1.5 million — Agency premium loss over 1,000-day window per travel role

**Appears in:** Article 23 (NurseSphere), nava-notebook, corpus-journal
**Source:** Article 23 links to cascadestaff.com as supporting authority. However: the Cascade article (cascadestaff.com/travel-nursing-and-prn-staffing-through-agencies/) does NOT contain this number. It is a general PRN vs. travel nursing explainer with no cost-per-role analysis.
**Derivation (inferred):** $1.5M over 1,000 days = $79,100/year × ~3 years (1,000 days ≈ 2.74 years). This matches if you annualize the $79,100 figure over a 3-year NurseSphere commitment.
**Verification:** 79,100 × 2.74 = ~$216,700/year × 2.74 years ≈ $593,000 — NOT $1.5M. So the $1.5M is NOT simply the annualized savings. It is a separate calculation that includes accumulated agency premiums, not just net savings vs. permanent hire cost.
**Full calculation (inferred):** 1,000 days of agency premiums = (agency hourly rate premium vs. permanent) × hours per year × 2.74 years. If the agency premium over a permanent RN is ~$20-25/hr, and the nurse works ~36hrs/week × 52 weeks = 1,872 hrs/year → $20 × 1,872 = $37,440/year premium. Over 2.74 years = $102,569. This is far short of $1.5M.
**Conclusion:** The $1.5M figure appears to include: agency fees + premium wages + re-onboarding costs + vacancy costs accumulated over 1,000 days of not converting. The article states it confidently but does not break it down. **This is a stated number without a published methodology.**
**Verdict:** Appears to be a cumulative multi-cost figure but methodology is not published. Requires primary research to confirm before external use.
