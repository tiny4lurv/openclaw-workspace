## Position Categories

### 🩺 Nursing (largest category)
The core of Nava's work — RNs, LPNs, CNAs at various levels and specialties.

**Most common roles:**
- **RN** (various: ADON, MDS, Unit Manager, Treatment, Supervisor, Discharge, Infusion, Wound Care)
- **DON** (Director of Nursing — most frequently listed leadership role)
- **LNHA** (Licensed Nursing Home Administrator)
- **LPN** (various shifts and settings)
- **CNA** (Certified Nursing Assistant — AM, PM, Night shifts)
- **ADON** (Assistant Director of Nursing)

**Settings seen:** skilled nursing facilities, assisted living, home health, hospitals, rehab centers

---

### 💊 Therapy & Rehabilitation

**Physical Therapy:** PT, PTA, COTA, PRN PT, FT PT, Travel PT
**Occupational Therapy:** OT, COTA, PRN OT, FT OT
**Speech-Language Pathology:** SLP, PRN SLP

Therapy roles are primarily PRN/per-diem and FT, with some travel contracts. Consistent demand across all three disciplines.

## Typical placement patterns

- **Perm placement:** DON, ADON, LNHA, Unit Manager, Leadership/Admin — mostly salaried FTE
- **PRN/per-diem:** CNAs, LPNs, RNs, Therapy (PT/OT/SLP) — per-shift or per-case
- **Travel/contract:** Multi-state entries, high volume in AL especially
- **Confidential searches:** DON searches often marked confidential with salary ranges ($120K-$150K for experienced DONs)

---

## Key takeaway

Nava's bread-and-butter is **nursing roles at all levels** (RN → DON being the hierarchy) in **skilled nursing / assisted living settings**. Therapy is steady PRN demand. Non-clinical is a real and growing part of the book — housekeeping, dietary, maintenance all present across multiple states. The geographic footprint is broad but concentrated in the Southeast + Midwest corridor.
