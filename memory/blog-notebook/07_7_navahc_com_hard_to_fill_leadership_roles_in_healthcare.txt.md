## The Problem This Article Addresses

Healthcare facilities are facing a structural vacancy crisis in leadership roles — not just a talent shortage, but a problem rooted in undefined mandates, informal succession planning, reliance on external hiring over internal development, and inadequate assessment methods for senior-level candidates.

## The Industry Practice Being Called Out

The "buy, not build" mindset in healthcare leadership hiring — defaulting to external searches for every senior role rather than investing in internal leadership development. This leaves organizations vulnerable, competing for the same scarce talent, raising costs, prolonging searches, and making retention harder. Also called out: posting senior roles generically on job boards, which only attracts active candidates and misses the passive leaders who would be ideal.

## Stakeholder Pain Points

**Healthcare Executives/C-Suite:**
- Future capability gaps, digital readiness, and leadership bench strength rank among top worries
- C-suite roles are shifting — consolidating responsibilities across operations, nursing, patient experience, and compliance, or creating new roles tied to analytics, automation, value-based care

**Board Members:**
- Succession planning failures have measurable financial and operational impact (Harvard Business School analysis on cost of poor succession planning)

**HR and Talent Leaders:**
- Difficulty attracting leaders with experience in multi-site operations, clinical quality, behavioral health management, digital modernization, and turnaround work
- Informal talent identification and last-minute hiring creating prolonged vacancies and costly mis-hires

**Incoming Leadership Candidates:**
- Senior leaders evaluate opportunities through one simple question: "What problem am I being asked to solve?" — they need precision, not vague role descriptions
- Mandate must include: strategic objectives for 12–24 months, decisions they will own, constraints they will inherit, degree of board/executive support

## Nava's Response

Nava Healthcare Recruitment positions itself as a **disciplined, market-aware, relationship-driven** partner for long-term clinical and operational placements. Their approach for hard-to-fill leadership roles includes:
1. **Deliberate prioritization** — classifying roles by strategic impact (clinical operations, quality, compliance, revenue cycle, nursing leadership, multi-site administration)
2. **Precision role definition** — co-creating mandates that include strategic objectives, decision ownership, constraints, and expected support structure
3. **Active, targeted sourcing** — recognizing strongest candidates are usually not in active search; using structured market maps, executive outreach, professional networks
4. **Mission and culture differentiation** — leveraging healthcare's mission-alignment weight (more than other industries) to attract leaders motivated by patient care impact
5. **Balanced capability building** — recommending external searches for specialized experience, but cross-functional rotations and stretch assignments for internal development

## What I Now Understand That I Didn't Before

1. Hard-to-fill roles are hard not just because talent is scarce, but because **roles themselves are shifting** — some consolidating responsibilities, others being newly created around analytics, automation, value-based care
2. The assessment bar for senior leaders must go **beyond traditional interviews** — behavioral evidence, scenario-driven discussions, psychometric evaluation, and multi-stakeholder reference checks
3. **Mission alignment matters more in healthcare than other sectors** — compensation needs to be competitive but doesn't determine outcome when the role influences patient care
4. The "buy, not build" default is a systemic issue, not just a talent problem — organizations have calcified around external hiring because internal development was never prioritized
