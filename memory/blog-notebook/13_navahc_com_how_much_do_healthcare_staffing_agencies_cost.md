## The Problem This Article Addresses

The article opens with a provocation: **"How much do healthcare staffing agencies charge?" is the wrong question.** The real cost of traditional staffing is not the fee percentage — it's the hidden costs of misaligned incentives, financial risk transfer to the client, and the transactional "placement first, retention never" philosophy that drives mis-hires and early turnover. The article argues that a facility paying a 20% contingency fee on a $61,000 RN turnover that happened at 90 days is not protected by the 90-day guarantee — they paid the full fee and still lost.

## The Industry Practice Being Called Out

Two dominant traditional fee models — **contingency** and **retained search** — both of which Nava argues are structurally misaligned with client success:

**Contingency Model (industry standard):**
- 15-30% of first-year salary, paid only upon successful placement
- Allows facilities to engage multiple agencies simultaneously for the same search
- Creates competitive environment where "numerous agencies are working" but incentivizes speed over quality
- The agency presents the "first available good candidate" not the "best possible candidate"
- The "no cure, no pay" framing masks significant strategic trade-offs

**Retained Search Model (executive standard):**
- 25-35% of first-year salary, paid in thirds (upfront, candidate slate, start date)
- Requires exclusivity and significant upfront investment
- More comprehensive and dedicated service, but client gives up competitive multi-agency engagement
- Reserved for high-level searches (CNO, DON) — not accessible for volume/floor staffing

The article calls out that **contingency models dependent on imprecise job boards** were designed to prioritize transaction over the client's long-term strategic goals of retention, quality, and cultural fit.

## Nava's Response

Nava proposes the **value-aligned partnership model** — a fundamental re-engineering of the client-agency relationship:

- **Zero upfront fees or retainers** — immediate demonstration of confidence in the vetting process
- **Retention-based fee structure** — full fee earned only after successful integration over a predetermined period
  - Floor staff: 30 days post-start
  - Management roles: 60 days post-start
- **Philosophical shift** from placement transaction to long-term strategic goal of performance and retention
- **Skin in the game** — Nava only wins when the client truly wins

The article makes clear this is the "definitive marker" of a true partnership: the agency's revenue is contingent on the hire succeeding over time, not just starting.
