## The Problem This Article Addresses

Healthcare facilities are paying premium rates ($90–$150/hr for temporary RNs vs. $35–$55/hr for permanent RNs) for a staffing model that doesn't actually solve their underlying instability. The "fast and flexible" model is making the crisis worse by burning out permanent staff, compromising patient safety, and creating a revolving door of poorly-vetted clinicians who churn within 90 days.

## The Industry Practice Being Called Out

The reactive, transactional "fast and flexible" staffing model — defined by speed-first sourcing, job board spam ("Post and Pray"), and a 90-day guarantee that acts as an accountability loophole. The industry defines "responsive" as finding the *available* clinician fastest, rather than finding the *adaptable* one. This commoditizes clinicians, ignores cultural fit, and perpetuates high turnover.

## Stakeholder Pain Points

**Healthcare Facility Leaders:**
- Financial volatility from premium temporary labor costs with no corresponding stability
- The 90-day guarantee is an accountability loophole — they absorb all unrecoverable costs (training, disrupted care, lost morale) when placements fail

**Permanent Staff (RNs, clinical teams):**
- Increased supervision and administrative burden of constantly orienting temporary staff
- Awareness of pay differentials between themselves and temporary hires
- Higher workload + perceived lower compensation = decreased morale and burnout

**High-Potential Candidates (passive, experienced clinicians):**
- Ignored by "Post and Pray" tactics — these clinicians are not actively job-hunting and won't respond to job board spam
- Treated as commodities rather than professionals with preferences and career goals

**Patients:**
- Compliance protocols are first casualty when units are short-staffed with poorly-vetted temps
- Missed protocols, outdated documentation, undertrained staff deployed to complex care environments

## Nava's Response

Nava Healthcare Recruitment operates a **proactive, relational "Human Intelligence" model** that:
1. **Rejects "Post and Pray"** — invests in proactive, high-volume outreach to passive candidates, building a proprietary pre-vetted database of adaptable professionals
2. **Solves structurally blind vetting** — uses rapport-based process to vet for "hidden flaws" (diligence, professionalism, EQ compatibility) that a transactional model cannot detect
3. **Aligns financial incentives with retention** — bills only after a clinician has been successfully on the job 30–60 days, putting their fee at risk and compelling proper vetting
4. **Facilitates temp-to-permanent conversions** — helps transition agency staff that facilities love into permanent employees
