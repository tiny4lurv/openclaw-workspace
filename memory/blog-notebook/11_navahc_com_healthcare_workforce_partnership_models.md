## The Problem This Article Addresses

Healthcare organizations face a **structural, not cyclical, workforce crisis** — persistent shortages, demographic shifts, burnout, wage compression, and new regulatory requirements have pushed traditional transactional hiring models past their limits. The article argues that reactive, siloed recruitment is architecturally incapable of producing the workforce stability that regulatory compliance now demands.

## The Industry Practice Being Called Out

The **transactional staffing model** — engaging agencies for one-off placements, relying on short-term travelers and locum tenens, treating recruitment as a procurement event rather than a strategic function. This approach creates 30-40% higher labor costs and does not build the consistent pipelines needed to meet new Joint Commission and CMS staffing standards.

## Stakeholder Pain Points

**Hospital Leaders:**
- New Joint Commission Nurse Staffing as National Performance Goal (effective 2026) makes workforce stability a compliance expectation, not an aspiration
- High nurse turnover rates create constant recruitment burden with no lasting solution
- Blended staffing models needed but difficult to operationalize without a dedicated partner

**Long-Term Care and Assisted Living Facilities:**
- CMS Nursing Home Staffing Campaign enforcing minimum staffing standards — non-compliance has regulatory consequences
- Need steady inflows of RNs, LPNs, CNAs, and support staff to maintain compliance and prevent staff burnout
- High overtime expenses from last-minute staffing to fill gaps

**Outpatient and Urgent Care Centers:**
- Unpredictable volumes make permanent headcount planning difficult
- Need flexible, credentialed coverage that can scale with seasonal peaks
- Extended hours strain understaffed teams

**Community Health and FQHCs:**
- Need blended workforce models (on-site, part-time, telehealth) to maintain access
- Budget constraints make premium labor costs unsustainable

**Healthcare HR/Recruitment Teams:**
- Credentialing delays are a known bottleneck impeding rapid, compliant onboarding
- No real-time visibility into pipeline health across departments
- Quarterly business reviews and SLA tracking absent from most staffing relationships

## Nava's Response

Nava positions **workforce partnership models** as the strategic alternative — moving recruitment from transactional placement to a continuous, integrated workforce function:

- **Multi-year workforce forecasting** using labor analytics, historical census patterns, turnover trends, and predictive modeling
- **Shared recruiting infrastructure** — not just filling roles but building pipelines
- **Integrated credentialing and compliance** — license verification, background checks, Joint Commission-aligned competency validation, automated renewal tracking
- **Predictable pipelines for high-need roles** across permanent, contract, temp-to-hire, and per-diem layers
- **Ongoing performance measurement** — SLAs, fill-rate and retention benchmarks, quarterly business reviews, candidate quality-score tracking
- **International recruitment pathways** (H-1B, EB-3) to address multi-year shortages through structured talent pipelines
- **Hybrid staffing layers** — permanent staff + partner-managed talent + technology working together under one coordinated model

**Benefits by Facility Type** are outlined across Hospitals, LTC/Assisted Living, Outpatient/Urgent Care, and Community Health/FQHCs.
